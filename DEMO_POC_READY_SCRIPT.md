# 30-Minute Demo Ready Script

## Summit Advisory Group POC

This file is meant to be used live during the session.

Use it as a direct speaking script.

Each section tells you:

1. what to open
2. what to show
3. what to say

The goal is to keep the demo high level, business-focused, and smooth.

## Demo Goal

By the end of 30 minutes, the audience should clearly understand that this POC already proves:

1. a real marketing site experience
2. reusable content-driven pages
3. multilingual readiness
4. search and content discovery
5. lead and subscriber capture
6. a credible path to Optimizely-backed publishing

## Demo Rule

Do not explain code unless someone asks.

Keep bringing the conversation back to:

- what the user can do
- what the business gains
- how the content model can scale

## 30-Minute Flow

| Time | What to Demo | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0:00 - 2:00 | Opening context | Frame the POC clearly |
| 2:00 - 6:00 | Homepage and navigation | Show the site as a real product |
| 6:00 - 10:00 | Service and industry pages | Show reusable business page models |
| 10:00 - 15:00 | Resource center and articles | Show content discovery and thought leadership flow |
| 15:00 - 20:00 | Search, contact, and subscription | Show real interaction and conversion |
| 20:00 - 24:00 | Localization | Show multilingual readiness |
| 24:00 - 27:00 | CMS preview and publishing concept | Show future-ready content operations story |
| 27:00 - 30:00 | Admin value and close | Show business usefulness and finish strong |

---

## 0:00 - 2:00

## Opening Context

### What to show

Start on the homepage:

`/en`

### What to say

> Thanks everyone for joining. Today I want to walk through the POC as a business-facing demo, not as a technical code review.

> The objective here was to build a realistic marketing-site foundation that can support structured pages, multilingual delivery, content discovery, lead capture, subscription flows, and a practical path toward CMS-managed publishing.

> So I will keep the session focused on functionality, user journeys, and business value.

### Transition line

> I will start with the website experience first, then show the interaction flows, and finally close with how this supports content operations and future production use.

---

## 2:00 - 6:00

## Homepage and Navigation

### What to show

Open:

`/en`

Scroll slowly through the homepage.

Use the header and navigation naturally.

### What to say

> This is the main landing experience. The important point is not only the UI itself, but that the page is built from structured content sections rather than being treated as a one-off page.

> That matters because it means the same platform can support different types of business pages in a consistent way.

> From a stakeholder point of view, this already feels like a real product site rather than a static prototype.

### What to point at while speaking

- page sections
- navigation
- calls to action
- overall polish and structure

### Short line for the audience

> The homepage is a good example of the POC already having the right business shape.

---

## 6:00 - 10:00

## Service and Industry Pages

### What to show

Open:

`/en/services/digital-platform-strategy`

Then open:

`/en/industries/healthcare-financial-resilience`

### What to say on the service page

> Here we move into a service-led page. This shows how the platform can present a specific offering with its own message, layout, and supporting content.

> The advantage is that business teams do not need every page to be custom-built separately. The model can support repeatable service pages with a consistent structure.

### What to say on the industry page

> This page shifts from service messaging to industry messaging. That is important because the business often needs to talk to the market from different angles: sometimes by service line, and sometimes by industry problem space.

> The POC already supports that variation while staying inside the same overall site model.

### Transition line

> So even at this level, we are already proving that the platform can support multiple business narratives, not just a single generic page template.

---

## 10:00 - 15:00

## Resource Center and Article Journey

### What to show

Open:

`/en/resource-center`

Then:

`/en/article/all`

Then open one article detail page, for example:

`/en/article/why-speed-is-the-missing-link-in-automotive-smart-factory-roi`

### What to say on the resource center page

> This is where the site starts to support thought leadership and ongoing content publishing, not just brochure-style pages.

> A business site becomes much more useful when it can organize insights, articles, and supporting content in one discoverable place.

### What to say on the article listing page

> This listing view demonstrates that the content model can scale. We are not limited to a few manually linked pages. We can present a growing library of content in a consistent way.

### What to say on the article detail page

> At the article level, the site becomes both a credibility tool and a conversion tool. Content here can educate users, build trust, and still connect them back into the broader journey through contact or subscription.

### Strong business line

> This is a meaningful step beyond a standard website demo because it shows how content can keep working after the homepage visit.

---

## 15:00 - 20:00

## Search, Contact, and Subscription

### What to show

First, if the search UI is stable, use search from the site header.

Then open:

`/en/contact`

Then open:

`/subscription`

### What to say before search

> Once the site grows, users should not have to rely only on navigation. Search becomes important because it turns the content library into something practical and usable.

### What to say on the contact page

> This page is where the POC moves from content consumption into conversion. A visitor can go from reading content to actually reaching out through a structured lead flow.

> That matters because it connects the marketing site to a real business outcome.

### If you submit a lead form live

Say this while filling it:

> I want to show that this is not only visual. The form flow is active, so the site can actually capture inquiries rather than simply display content.

### What to say on the subscription page

> This is a second engagement path. Instead of only capturing direct leads, the site can also capture audience interest through subscription.

> That gives the business another way to build ongoing engagement rather than treating every visit as a one-time event.

### If you submit a subscription live

Say this:

> This is useful because it shows the POC can support both direct conversion and audience nurturing.

### Transition line

> Up to this point, we have already seen page rendering, content discovery, and real interaction flows working together.

---

## 20:00 - 24:00

## Localization

### What to show

Open:

`/es`

Then:

`/es/resource-center`

Then:

`/es/contact`

### What to say

> Another important part of the POC is multilingual readiness. The same site model can support localized delivery without requiring a separate build approach for each region.

> From a business perspective, this matters for scale, consistency, and market reach.

> It also shows that localization is part of the overall content structure, not something bolted on later.

### Short line for emphasis

> The value here is not just translation. The value is that the platform is already structured for multi-region publishing.

---

## 24:00 - 27:00

## CMS Preview and Publishing Concept

### What to show

If already validated before the call, briefly show preview behavior.

If not, do not force a live preview.

Keep the current browser page open while explaining the concept.

### What to say if you are only explaining conceptually

> The final piece is the content operations story. A business site like this becomes much more valuable when editors can preview and publish changes through a CMS workflow instead of depending on manual page changes for everything.

> The important point in this POC is that the front-end rendering flow is already designed to work with that kind of CMS-backed content model.

> So even if I keep this demo focused on the stable product journey, the architecture is already aligned with Optimizely-style preview and publishing flows.

### What to say if preview is working and you show it

> Here I am showing the preview concept briefly. This demonstrates how content updates can be reviewed before they are treated as published site content.

> That is important because it reduces dependence on technical teams for routine editorial changes.

### Keep this section high level

Do not drift into:

- environment variables
- raw API calls
- secrets
- troubleshooting

---

## 27:00 - 30:00

## Admin Value and Strong Close

### What to show

If available and safe, open:

`/admin/leads?key=YOUR_ADMIN_KEY`

If useful, mention CSV export availability.

### What to say

> One final piece of value is that the POC is not only collecting interactions, it can also support the operational side of those interactions.

> For example, lead and subscriber information can be reviewed or exported, which makes the website more useful to the business beyond the front-end experience itself.

### Final close

Use this closing statement directly:

> To summarize, this POC already proves the end-to-end business flow. We have structured marketing pages, reusable content patterns, multilingual support, content discovery, lead capture, subscription, and a clear path toward Optimizely-backed publishing.

> So the value here is not just that we built pages. The value is that we now have a practical foundation for a real CMS-driven marketing platform.

## Backup Lines for Q and A

### If someone asks what is the biggest value of the POC

Say:

> The biggest value is that it brings together content presentation, interaction, multilingual delivery, and business conversion in one consistent platform.

### If someone asks why this matters for business teams

Say:

> It gives business teams a site structure that can support campaigns, services, thought leadership, and lead generation without rebuilding the experience from scratch each time.

### If someone asks whether this is only a front-end exercise

Say:

> No. The important point is that this POC already supports real user journeys and operational value, not just visual rendering.

### If someone asks what comes next after this POC

Say:

> The next step would be production hardening: aligning final CMS authoring needs, validating deployment and governance, and preparing the operational rollout.

## Final Delivery Notes

### During the session

- speak slowly
- do not rush transitions
- keep each explanation short
- always connect the feature to business value
- avoid technical detail unless asked directly

### Most important presenter mindset

Present this as an owner explaining a solution.

Do not present it as an intern showing a project for approval.

### Final sentence to remember

> This POC already demonstrates the business shape of the solution, not just the technical possibility of it.
